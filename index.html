How it works

Modulation: 8-FSK in the 1200–1900 Hz audible band. Each 50 ms symbol carries 3 bits. A 500 ms chirp sweep (500–2500 Hz) marks the start.

Frame: [chirp preamble] [0xAA 0x55] [len] [URL bytes] [CRC-16] [0x55 0xAA], transmitted twice for redundancy.

Why audible band: FM radio caps audio at 15 kHz; AM caps at 5–10 kHz; podcast codecs (Opus, MP3 at 128 kbps) preserve the speech band (1–4 kHz) best. Putting tones at 1.2–1.9 kHz survives all of these.

Why direct URL encoding: Avoids the Chirp/Sonos shortcode-to-server patent (active until 2032). No server lookup; the URL travels in the audio.

What this does not do yet: No cryptographic signing, no Reed-Solomon FEC (CRC only), no analytics, no app. v1 prototype.
