How AudioQR v2.1 works

Modulation: 8-FSK in the 1200 to 1900 Hz audible band. Each 55 ms symbol carries 3 bits, with a 10 ms guard between symbols. A 500 ms chirp sweep (500 to 2500 Hz) marks the start.

Frame: [chirp] [training tones 0-7] [0xAA 0x55 0xC3] [len] [payload] [CRC-16] [0xC3 0x55 0xAA], transmitted twice. The training preamble lets the decoder learn per-tone gain on the actual mic and room channel before reading the payload.

Error tolerance: Per-symbol micro-realignment with drift tracking, byte-level majority voting across both copies, and CRC-16 validation. No false-positive CRC search.

Short tokens: 6-character Crockford Base32 (excludes I, L, O, U to avoid ambiguity). About 1.07 billion combinations. Broadcasts in roughly 7 to 8 seconds. The listener resolves the token to a full URL via tokens.json on this site.

Why audible band: FM radio caps audio at 15 kHz, AM caps at 5 to 10 kHz, podcast codecs preserve the speech band (1 to 4 kHz) best. Putting tones at 1.2 to 1.9 kHz survives all of these.

Why a registry (not direct shortcode lookup): The decoded payload is the token itself. The listener performs a static JSON fetch from a public file. No proprietary lookup server, no patent exposure.
